# Proposed Taxonomy

## US Politics
- **Presidential Elections**: Outcomes of US Presidential Elections, popular vote, inauguration, and state-level results.
- **Nominations & Primaries**: Questions related to party nominations for presidential/VP candidates and candidate dropouts.
- **Policy & Governance**: Legislative actions, government shutdowns, legal processes involving political figures, public statements by politicians within a policy context.
- **Appointments & Personnel**: Forecasts regarding nominations and confirmations for federal positions, including the Federal Reserve.

## World Politics & Geopolitics
- **National Elections (Non-US)**: Presidential, Prime Minister, or parliamentary elections occurring outside the United States.
- **International Relations & Conflicts**: Military actions, ceasefires, diplomatic events, changes in foreign leadership, and inter-country relations.

## Economy & Finance
- **Monetary Policy**: Decisions and statements from central banks, primarily the Federal Reserve, concerning interest rates.
- **Macroeconomics**: Forecasts on broad economic indicators such as inflation rates, jobs reports, and national debt.
- **Financial Markets**: Price movements or performance of indices, commodities, and other traditional financial assets.

## Sports
- **NFL**: Outcomes of Super Bowls, specific game results, player awards, and team/player-related events in the National Football League.
- **NBA**: Outcomes of NBA Finals, specific game results, and team/player-related events in the National Basketball Association.
- **Global Soccer**: Winners of major leagues (e.g., EPL, La Liga), cup competitions (e.g., Champions League), and specific match results.
- **Combat Sports**: Outcomes of professional boxing and mixed martial arts (MMA) matches.
- **Other Sports**: Events and outcomes for all other sports including NHL, MLB, college sports, international basketball, darts, tennis, F1, and multi-sport events like the Olympics.

## Crypto
- **Price Predictions**: Forecasts on the future value of cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and altcoins.
- **Protocol & Launches**: Events related to new token launches, airdrops, NFT floor prices, and crypto platform volumes.
- **Regulation & Policy**: Government actions, legislation, or bans specifically targeting cryptocurrencies and blockchain technology.

## Culture & Media
- **Movies, TV & Awards**: Box office performance, success of films and television shows, and outcomes of major awards ceremonies (e.g., Oscars, Emmys).
- **Social Media & Internet**: Events related to major social media platforms, tech company leadership changes, and internet policy (e.g., app bans).
- **Gaming & Pop Culture**: Outcomes of eSports tournaments, celebrity-related events (not explicitly political), and general entertainment topics.

## Science & Environment
- **Space & AI**: Developments in space exploration, artificial intelligence, software releases, and general technology breakthroughs.
- **Weather & Natural Disasters**: Predictions for local temperatures, severe weather events, and natural phenomena like earthquakes.

## Miscellaneous
- **Unclassified**: Markets that do not fit into any other category, typically novelty or pure chance events.

---

## Rationale
The taxonomy prioritizes forecaster recognition and concrete topics over abstract ones, as requested. It balances broad coverage with specific splits for high-volume domains like Sports, US Politics, and Crypto, ensuring distinct subcategories for major leagues, political processes, and crypto market segments. The addition of "Science & Environment" captures recurring, albeit lower-volume, topics like weather and AI, preventing a large "Other" bucket. The category and subcategory limits are adhered to, with "Other Sports" serving as a necessary catch-all to keep the main sports categories focused.

## Coverage concerns
The interpretation of public statements by political figures (e.g., Trump saying "FEMA" or Kamala Harris saying "black" during a livestream) could sometimes blur the line between "US Politics -> Policy & Governance" and "Culture & Media -> Gaming & Pop Culture" (if viewed purely as celebrity utterances). I've leaned towards "US Politics" given the inherent political context of the speakers. The "Miscellaneous" category currently only holds the "Coin Toss" question, which is genuinely unclassifiable elsewhere.
